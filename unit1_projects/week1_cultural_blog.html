ValuesApril 27th, 2014
When you think of the times in your life where you've been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, what values come to mind?

Achievement Accountability Adventure Affection Autonomy Challenge Close Relationships Commitment Community Compassion Confidence Cooperation Creativity Economic security Environmentalism Fairness Friendships Having a family Health Helping other people Honesty Insight Integrity Introspection Meaningful work Nature Personal development Self-reliance Self-respect

It is through Introspection that I'm able to place myself in the context of the world. Many of the other values are important but introspection is what strengthens them. For me, it provides a better understanding of things. It helps with the most difficult situations I've faced and has made the enjoyable ones even more worthwhile.



The personal importance of living up to these values:

Extremely Important (5): Accountability, Commitment, Compassion, Creativity, Health, Helping Other People, Honesty, Integrity, Introspection, Personal Development, Self-Respect

Very Important (4): Achievement, Affection, Autonomy, Close Relationships, Community, Confidence, Environmentalism, Fairness, Friendships

Important (3): Adventure, Challenge, Cooperation, Economic Security, Having a Family, Insight, Nature, Self-Reliance

Slightly Important (2): Meaningful Work

Not very important (1):

What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on?:
How to better socialize in a new city. I'm not the best socializer myself, so I've thought out the overwhelming number of hardships the task presents.
